March 2026

Representing Transcard at the UTC Career Fair

I had the opportunity to table at UTC's CECS Career Fair on behalf of Transcard. It was amazing to speak to upcoming young professionals — I know exactly what it's like to be in their shoes. I've been to the career fair three times: the first two as a student and CECS Ambassador, helping set up the fair and participating to land an internship.

My first career fair, I learned how to talk to recruiters and what they were looking for. My second, I interviewed with Transcard and earned the IT Infrastructure internship. My third, I tabled and spoke to students about opportunities at Transcard. Truly thankful to have played all three sides: Student, Ambassador, and Representative.

January 2026

Accepting a Full-Time Role at Transcard

I'm excited to share that I've officially accepted a full-time System Administrator position at Transcard, starting January 5th.

What started as an internship has grown into an opportunity to continue learning, building, and supporting critical systems alongside an incredible infrastructure team. I'm grateful for the trust, mentorship, and support that made this next step possible. Thank you to everyone at Transcard and UTC who played a role in this journey.

September 2025

Summer Internship Wrap-Up & Return Offer

September 2025

Why I Founded My Fraternity Chapter

Before I was a brother, I was coasting through college with no particular direction. That changed the moment I was presented with the opportunity to become a Founding Brother of the Epsilon Beta chapter of Lambda Theta Phi at UTC. I didn't just want to join a brotherhood — I wanted to commit to transforming and growing as a man.

I started showing up and being intentional. I ran for leadership after crossing into the brotherhood and was elected President of our newly founded chapter. My resume grew. More importantly, so did I. I gained confidence, started applying for scholarships I'd normally pass on, and became an HSF Scholar. My chapter was honored with a Proclamation from the City of Chattanooga for our service and community impact.

I leaned into campus resources and got connected with Matt Solima at Transcard. That connection opened the door to a job shadow, then an internship — all because I stepped out of my comfort zone. None of this would've happened if I hadn't taken that first step. If you're feeling stuck: take one step. Get involved. Be intentional. You never know how far it can take you.

December 2024

First IT Role: UTC Help Desk Technician

I accepted a position within UTC's IT Department as an IT Help Desk Technician — my first professional role in IT. Entering my senior year, this opportunity was pivotal in breaking into the field and building foundational experience.

  • Supported approximately 11,000 students, 728 faculty/staff, and 35,000 alumni
  • Troubleshot hardware, software, and networking issues across Windows and macOS
  • Provided phone and remote support in a fast-paced academic environment
  • Assisted with password resets, email configuration, and access to university systems
  • Communicated technical solutions clearly to users of all experience levels

September 2025

Entry #3 — Why I Founded My Fraternity Chapter

Before I was a brother, I was coasting through college with no particular direction. That changed the moment I was presented with the opportunity to become a Founding Brother of the Epsilon Beta chapter of Lambda Theta Phi at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga. I didn't just want to join a brotherhood — I wanted to commit myself to transform and grow as a man.

I started showing up and being intentional. I put time into meeting brothers from different backgrounds, learning from them, and building connections. I got my first campus job and gained real responsibilities. After crossing into the brotherhood, I ran for leadership and was elected President of our newly founded chapter. My resume grew — and more importantly, so did I. I gained confidence, started applying for scholarships I'd normally pass on, and became an HSF Scholar.

My chapter dedicated itself to serving the community both on and off campus. With our combined effort, we were honored with a Proclamation from the City of Chattanooga recognizing our dedication to service. I took my professional development more seriously, became an IT Help Desk Technician at UTC, and joined CECS as an ambassador.

I leaned into campus resources, joined the Career Center email list, and got paired with Matt Solima from Transcard. That connection opened the door to a job shadowing opportunity with the IT Security team — and later, an internship with IT Infrastructure — all because I stepped out of my comfort zone at the career fair.

None of this would've happened if I hadn't taken that first step. Founding the Epsilon Beta chapter was more than bringing a fraternity to campus — it was about taking ownership of my life, choosing growth over comfort, and building something from the ground up for myself, my brothers, and future Latino students searching for purpose and a place to belong. If you're feeling stuck: take one step. Get involved. Be intentional. You never know how far it can take you.

Entry #1 — First IT Role: UTC Help Desk Technician

I accepted a position within the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga's IT Department as an IT Help Desk Technician — my first professional role in IT. Entering my senior year, this opportunity was pivotal in breaking into the field and building foundational experience.

  • Supported approximately 11,000 students, 728 faculty/staff, and 35,000 alumni
  • Troubleshot hardware, software, and networking issues across Windows and macOS systems
  • Provided phone and remote support in a fast-paced academic environment
  • Assisted with password resets, email configuration, and access to university systems
  • Communicated technical solutions clearly to users of all experience levels
